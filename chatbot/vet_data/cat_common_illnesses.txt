Common Cat Illnesses:

Upper Respiratory Infections (URIs):
Symptoms: Sneezing, runny nose, watery eyes, coughing, lethargy, fever, loss of appetite.
Causes: Usually viral (e.g., feline herpesvirus, calicivirus) or bacterial.
Treatment: Supportive care, antibiotics if bacterial, humidifiers. Keep infected cats isolated.

Urinary Tract Issues (e.g., Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease - FLUTD):
Symptoms: Straining to urinate, frequent urination, blood in urine, urinating outside the litter box, crying during urination.
Causes: Stress, diet, bladder stones, infections, idiopathic cystitis.
Treatment: Varies by cause; may include diet change, medication, stress reduction. Urethral obstruction is an emergency.

Parasites:
Fleas: Itching, hair loss, skin irritation. Can transmit tapeworms.
Ticks: Can transmit diseases like Lyme disease (less common in cats than dogs but possible).
Worms (Roundworms, Hookworms, Tapeworms): Vomiting, diarrhea, weight loss, pot-belly appearance.
Prevention: Regular flea/tick preventatives and deworming as recommended by your vet.
